# Paper Summary

## Title
A Game-Theoretic Analysis of Fare Competition Between JoyRide and MoveIt in Malolos City: A Non-Zero-Sum Duopoly Approach

## Abstract
This study develops and analyzes a game-theoretic model of fare competition between two motorcycle ride-hailing platforms — JoyRide and MoveIt — operating in Malolos City, Bulacan. Survey data from 60 BeSU college students calibrate a 3×3 non-zero-sum payoff matrix. Two asymmetric pure-strategy Nash equilibria are identified: (H, M) and (M, H), each yielding 25,200 profit units per firm. A mixed-strategy equilibrium exists at Pr(H) ≈ 74.3%.

## Equilibrium Results

### Pure-Strategy Nash Equilibria (2 equilibria)
- **(H, M)**: JoyRide → High Fare, MoveIt → Medium Fare → π_A = π_B = 25,200
- **(M, H)**: JoyRide → Medium Fare, MoveIt → High Fare → π_A = π_B = 25,200

### Mixed-Strategy Nash Equilibrium
- σ* = (Pr(H) = 52/70 ≈ 74.3%, Pr(M) = 18/70 ≈ 25.7%)
- Expected payoff ≈ 23,863 units per firm

## Key Findings
1. Discounted Fare (D) is strictly dominated by both H and M → eliminated by IESDS
2. Fare **differentiation** (not uniform pricing) is the stable, profit-maximizing outcome
3. Survey perception data supports the equilibrium: JoyRide is seen as safer (supports H), MoveIt as more affordable (supports M)
4. The mixed equilibrium explains observed promotional cycling behavior
